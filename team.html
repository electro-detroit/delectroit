Core team

BG
Bryttany Grimes (she/her)
Researcher — History & gender studies

Second‑year PhD student in the Department of History. Bryttany's work centers on Nigeria, gender, Islam, and British colonialism; her current research examines how Hausa women use dress to express internal valuations of womanhood in response to Islam, colonial rule, and Western influence.

AI
Ayaat Ismail (she/her)
Researcher — English & cultural studies

English Ph.D. student at Michigan State University focusing on 21st‑century Palestinian cultural production, with particular interest in portrayals of Palestinians in literature and film. Her work examines memory, surveillance, infrastructure, carcerality, and expressions of sumud.

HF
Harry M. Foster (he/him)
Researcher — English & cultural studies

PhD student in the Department of English at Michigan State University. He completed his undergraduate degree in English and Creative Writing at Columbia University and served five years in the Marines. His research examines expressions and manifestations of cultural heroism across folklore, literature, film, and comics from the 19th century to the present, with particular interest in how heroic archetypes perform resistance and survival strategies while navigating systemic oppression. He also publishes creative work alongside his academic research.
